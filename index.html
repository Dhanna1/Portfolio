Mechanical design, electronics, embedded software, testing and continuous improvement.

I’m Dalton Hanna, a mechatronics engineering graduate focused on robotics, automation, embedded systems and product development. What excites me most is taking an idea through the entire process: defining the problem, designing the mechanics, integrating electronics, developing the controls and testing the real hardware until it becomes a working system.

EDUCATIONB.S. Mechatronics Engineering
FOCUSRobotics, controls and automation
APPROACHDesign, build, test and improve

I enjoy working across mechanical, electrical and software boundaries because that is where the most interesting engineering decisions happen. A CAD model must leave room for wiring. Firmware must account for real sensor noise. A mechanism must be serviceable after assembly. My projects have taught me to judge a design by how the complete system behaves—not by whether each individual component works on its own.

01 / COMPLETE SYSTEMS

Carry the idea through.

I prefer projects where I can contribute from the initial concept and CAD work through fabrication, electronics, programming, troubleshooting and final operation.

02 / ITERATIVE DESIGN

Use testing as evidence.

The first working version is a checkpoint, not the finish line. Real testing reveals packaging, control, reliability and usability constraints that cannot always be predicted beforehand.

03 / REAL HARDWARE

Design for physical behavior.

Motors behave differently, sensors produce noise, wires need space and printed parts have tolerances. I use calibration and hands-on troubleshooting to connect software decisions to real machine behavior.

04 / CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT

Always identify Version 2.

Every finished project creates a clearer next step. I naturally look for ways to make systems more reliable, easier to assemble, easier to maintain and better suited for continued development.

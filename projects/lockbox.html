Timed lockbox

PROJECT OVERVIEW

A focus product designed to create a physical barrier.

The device allows the user to choose a lock duration, starts a countdown and physically restricts access until the timer expires.

LONGEST TEST3 hoursContinuous locked timer

SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

INPUTS

Select duration and begin the lock cycle.

→

→
OUTPUTS

LCD + servo lock

Countdown feedback and physical actuation.

REQUIREMENTS & CONSTRAINTS

Simplicity, cost and physical reliability were prioritized.

USER INTERFACE

Fast time selection

A potentiometer and push button avoided the complexity and cost of a keypad.

MECHANISM

Low-cost servo lock

A servo provided sufficient torque and simple PWM control.

ENCLOSURE

Thermal and component packaging

I designed the printed enclosure around the LCD, controls, wiring, servo and ventilation openings.

DEVELOPMENT TIMELINE

Build the electronics, prove the timing, then package the product.

01

Electronic proof of concept

Validated the potentiometer, push button, display and servo independently.

02

Non-blocking countdown

Implemented the timer using millis() so the display and system state could continue updating.

03

Input filtering

Stabilized time selection and prevented analog jitter from changing the requested duration.

04

Custom enclosure

Designed and printed the final housing with ventilation and integrated controls.

05

Repeated testing

Verified lock/unlock operation, countdown accuracy and ten continuous successful cycles.

PROBLEMS & ITERATIONS

The most important failure mode appeared after the product worked.

01

Lid geometry

The lid functioned but should have been redesigned to fit the locking mechanism more precisely.

02

Battery inaccessible while locked

If the battery died in the locked state, power could not be restored from outside. A future version needs an externally accessible battery or emergency power connection.

03

Servo durability

The mechanism worked reliably during testing, but long-term wear was not characterized.

PRODUCT GALLERY

Finished enclosure and internal packaging.

FINAL RESULTS

Reliable locking, accurate feedback and a three-hour validated cycle.

The device locked and unlocked consistently, displayed an accurate countdown and maintained stable time selection after input filtering. It completed ten continuous cycles without failure and was tested at a maximum duration of three hours.

Version 2: Redesign the lid interface, provide external battery or emergency power access, improve the locking system's durability and refine enclosure aesthetics.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

A programmable motion product designed for repeatable interaction.

The automated cat laser used servo-driven motion patterns, reliable on/off control and a direct programming connection that allowed additional behavior to be loaded later.

DESIGN PROCESS

Turn basic servo movement into a reusable behavior platform.

01

Created a servo-driven assembly capable of directing the laser over an interaction area.

02

Developed engaging motion sequences and effective switching between behaviors.

FINAL PROTOTYPE

Enclosed mechanism with a visible servo-mounted laser assembly.

FINAL RESULTS

Consistent controls and engaging programmable motion.

The system worked as intended. Switching on and off was dependable, movement patterns were engaging and transitions between them were effective. The programming connection also made the product extensible rather than fixed to one behavior.

Future version: Improve enclosure finish, protect the external mechanism, add adjustable movement boundaries and include an automatic play-duration shutoff.

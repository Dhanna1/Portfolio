03 · CALIBRATE WALL DETECTION

Threshold and averaging tests

Adjusted analog thresholds and sampling behavior to distinguish open paths from walls.

04 · ADD WALL CENTERING

Used wall readings to correct position while driving, reducing collisions and helping the robot remain aligned.

PROBLEMS & ITERATIONS

The project succeeded, but it also exposed the limits of open-loop control.

01

Repeated left turns

The robot could normally complete paths containing one left turn, but paths with multiple left turns often prevented a successful finish. This was the primary performance limitation.

02

Motor mismatch

Equal PWM commands did not produce equal wheel speeds, requiring independent tuning values.

03

Wall collisions

Timed forward movement alone was not accurate enough. Continuous wall-centering correction produced the biggest improvement.

04

Calibration ownership

Generated code could not solve the physical calibration problem. Sensor thresholds and motor behavior had to be adjusted manually through repeated testing.

FINAL RESULTS

Four autonomous runs and a measurable control improvement.

The finished robot navigated completely autonomously, completed four successful runs and reached the maze goal several times. Wall-centering control was the most meaningful improvement because it converted sensor readings into continuous motion correction rather than simple obstacle detection.

Version 2: Matched motors, encoder feedback, closed-loop turns, cleaner modular wiring and a mapped maze strategy would directly address the repeated-left-turn limitation.
